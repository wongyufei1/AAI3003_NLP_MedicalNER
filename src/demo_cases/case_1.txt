Mr. James Anderson, a 58-year-old male, visited complaining of occasional headaches and fatigue, particularly in the mornings. His history revealed a family predisposition to hypertension, with his father having had a stroke. Mr. Anderson admitted to a sedentary lifestyle and a diet high in sodium, contributing factors to his condition. Following assessment, a diagnosis of essential hypertension was confirmed, prompting recommendations for lifestyle modifications including dietary changes, regular exercise, and stress management techniques.
