Mr. Michael Brown, a 45-year-old male, complained of recurrent heartburn and regurgitation, particularly after meals and while lying down. His symptoms had worsened over months, impacting his quality of life. With no significant weight loss or dysphagia, a diagnosis of gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) was established, guiding treatment with proton pump inhibitors, lifestyle modifications, and regular follow-up to assess symptom improvement.
